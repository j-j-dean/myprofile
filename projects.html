Gamma Ray Observatory

I came on board this project a year before launch of the satellite. Most of the software to support spacecraft operations were complete. I learned how to work with a team to correct discrepencies. I learned about the software life cycle through experience and through on site training classes. I was responsible for implementing new requirements and updating the corresponding documentation for the user (user's guide and design documents). I was instrumental in developing software to perform limit testing allowing the flight operations team to monitor the health and safety of the spacecraft. The software was developed in FORTRAN using a Perkin-Elmer main frame computer. On site at NASA we had removable disc packs that housed the software for various spacecraft that were scheduled around the clock to make use of limited resources.

Wind/Polar

This was a great project to work on. I was given the responsibility to develop the command system software for the satellite control center. This included verifying the user request and obtaining the related command data from the database using SQL. I became immersed in learning and understanding the new way to develop the ground system satellite systems using SGI workstations, commercial and in-house common code and developing the software using the C programming language. As such, I earned the role as integrator for the WIND/POLAR task. WIND and POLAR are two distinct satellites with some very common science tasks being shared between them, so they were developed together. However, they were delivered separately and the launch of the satellites was about a year apart. As integrator I was responsible for preparing a delivery solution to our NASA customer that would allow all of the custom software, generic software (between various satellite systems), and commercial software to be packaged for a smooth delivery to our internal test team, the NASA test team, and the flight operations team. Additionally, I attended requirement, design and code reviews for the custom software, and the common software. The entire development life cycle was important to our company and our NASA client in creating the requested solution. I also worked with our team to support data flows with the flight operations team and the external simulator. Throughout multiple delivery cycles I was tasked with determining the cost and effort required to implement new requests.

SOHO

While supporting WIND/POLAR as they approached launch I began working part time on the SOHO satellite system. After a major change in personnel I was tasked with integrating this system for delivery to the NASA customer. I also found solutions to process telemetry data from 9mm tape as required and providing a 1750 floating point conversion utility. I was responsible for integrating the SOHO software, the generic software, and commercial software for delivery to our NASA customer. I filled a critical role on this team allowing them to deliver their software release on time.

VBS Sets and Staging

As a result of performing many DIY projects I have become a little handy at making things with my hands. Our church began using sponsored curriculm from various organizations to host our summer Vacation Bible School event. I assisted by being a leader in creating the set and staging for the event. Pictured are a only a few of the items that we have used over the years. I created a portion of the items at home and then delivered them to church. Many of the items created are originals. I worked with the ministry team to create items that were relevant to our theme (Norway, Thailand, Africa, Weird Animals, Master Fun Factory,...). I created reusable panels to display a backdrop that has improved efficiency and lowered costs. As you might imagine there is not a big budget for this activity, so it is imperative to create staging that is relevant to our theme and within budget. As the event approaches my role has needed to be flexible to work with anyone who shows up to help. This often includes students, youth, parents and even grandparents. So, it becomes necessary to find jobs that meet the skills of the helper (painting, light carpentry, operating equipment, ladder work). It is a joy to be a part of this team that is creative and making a difference in our community.
