And...so much more... This is a very challenging job in so many ways. I wouldn't change my time in this role for anything. The reward of helping my children grow and being around during those years has been the greatest blessing I can imagine. I am ever grateful for this experience.

I began my career working on NASA's Gamma Ray Observatory whose role was to study the effects of Gamma Rays in the near Earth orbit. We worked closely with the NASA customer to identify requirements, to design systems to accomplish the task, to develop software in FORTRAN and to perform comprehensive testing to ensure a quality product.

On subsequent projects I worked on the WIND/POLAR and SOHO spacecrafts that were developed on SGI workstations using C and C++. During those projects I became instrumental in providing guidance and instructions to providing a smooth integration of all the software and components. My role was elevated to lead integrator to provide delivery of those projects to the customer in addition to being responsible for designing/developing software to send commands to the spacecraft.
